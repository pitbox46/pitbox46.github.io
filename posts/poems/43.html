In the silver plane I see a boy Curled up, shivering Wasting and withering More destitute than my last view Less stable than my first In the silver I see him Chasing threads, running Finishing and falling More confused than my last view Less focused than my first I wish he could hear me My calls, my cries My warnings and compliments I watch horrified As he climbs the wrong peaks As he falls with no reasons
